Skipper

Benefit from the many years of experience of the German skipper Alexander Schmid and experience an unforgettable adventure with him - the drill of a bluefin tuna, the largest and most powerful of all tuna.

Alexander's enthusiasm for fishing was sparked at the age of four. He is a passionate fly fisherman and has extensive big game experience in the Atlantic, Pacific and Indian Oceans. Since 2002 he has lived with his family in Istria and successfully fishes in the Adriatic.

Successes

His quality and professionalism are proven by his win of the Istrian Big Game Championship in 2019. The German sea fishing magazine "Kutter und Costa" reported on it. Furthermore, he achieved medal places in various fishing competitions.

Book your adventure and experience an adrenaline-filled day together with the Istrian master in big game fishing.

Reference

Alexander is a sought-after guide! He fishes successfully on an 11m boat licensed by the ICCAT and annually catches the quota of 6 tons of bluefin tuna approved by the ICCAT with rod and reel.

Spinning for tuna

With the spinning rod for bluefin tuna - a fantastic experience! Experience the moment when a swarm of tuna breaks through the surface of the water in a feeding frenzy, a tuna attacks your spinning lure in front of your eyes and after a successful strike the fish makes your spinning reel screaming.

In spring, a lot of feed fish is concentrated off the coast of Istria and due to the shallow water depth of only 40m on average, schools of tuna drive their prey fish to the surface and form large feeding frencies - a special position of Istria compared to other destinations.

Drift fishing for tuna

Bluefin tuna can be caught almost all year round off Istria. Drift fishing with natural bait is by far the most successful technique for catching bluefins in the Adriatic. The fish are attracted to the boat by chunking and chumming. This method brings the best results all year round. Only in June the bluefin tunas leave the Adriatic to congregate in the spawning areas. At the beginning of July, after this exhausting journey, the fish return to the north of the Adriatic to eat themselves fat on the large stocks of sardines. A large number of sardine trawlers operate here, which use powerful reflectors to attract plankton and thereby swarms of sardines. This also attracts the tuna.

Giant tuna fishing

Autumn is the time of the Giants. Large schools of sardines are then concentrated on site and with you large schools of tuna, including the very large ones. Fish weighing over 200kg are caught here every year.

Fishing area

The region of Istria in northern Croatia can be reached quickly due to its proximity to neighboring European countries and the good infrastructure and is therefore predestined for a holiday in Croatia.

Crystal clear water, dreamy bathing bays, picturesque coastal towns, historical sights and unspoilt nature make Istria one of the most popular holiday regions in Croatia, not only because of the large tuna fish.

Our base is in Pula. Due to the strategic location in the south of Istria, we fish depending on where the tuna are on the east, south or west side of the peninsula and relocate our boat accordingly.
